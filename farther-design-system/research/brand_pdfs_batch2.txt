===== Farther BG_18_Messaing_Implementation.pdf =====
IMPLEMENTATION
Capitalization
and punctuation
Sentence case throughout — Signals modern, accessible, not corporate-formal

Title case reserved for data viz, tables, reports — Creates visual hierarchy

No periods in headlines (unless multiple sentences) — Keeps energy forward

"And" not "&" wherever space allows — Reads more humanized, less corporate

Oxford commas in lists — Clarity wins over style

Em dashes (—) with spaces for emphasis — Reads more sophisticated

En dashes (–) without spaces for number ranges — 2024–2026, not hyphen

Hanging punctuation for inline quotes, numbered list, and bullet lists

Numeric abbreviations: T, B, M, k — Clean, modern (not "Trillion," "Billion," etc.)

===== Farther BG_19_Messaing_Implementation.pdf =====
Messaging 	IMPLEMENTATION
Inclusive language When referring to individuals and groups, we always use their preferred language. Use
LGBTQ (or most current)

When referring to a group, use non-gendered identifiers (e.g., folks or everybody)

Use “woman” and “women” instead of “female” and “man” or “men” instead of “male”

When featuring an individual, always defer to how they self identify

Only identify a person’s race, ethnicity, or national origin if it is relevant

Capitalize and do not pluralize Black

When referring to those with disabilities, emphasize the person first (e.g., “A person with a
disability” not “disabled person” or “handicapped person”)


Also be sure to research the origin of any idiom before using it in copy.
